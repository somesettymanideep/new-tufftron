What is Toughened Glass?

Toughened glass (also known as tempered glass) is a type of safety glass that has been specially treated to be stronger than regular glass. Itâ€™s made by heating the glass to very high temperatures and then cooling it rapidly, making it more challenging. The outer surface is compressed, while the inside remains under tension, giving it increased strength.

One of the most significant advantages of toughened glass is its safety features. When it breaks, it shatters into small, blunt pieces, significantly reducing the risk of injury. This safety aspect makes it a popular choice for a wide range of applications in both residential and commercial settings, providing a sense of security and protection.
